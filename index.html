Stock Information

India has six major operating stock exchanges, with the Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) and National Stock Exchange (NSE) being the most prominent.International exchanges (India INX, NSE IX) and specialized ones (MSE, USE, MCX, NCDEX, ICEX) broaden the scope to global and commodity trading.

Key Stock Exchanges in India

  1. Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE)
    • Founded in 1875,headquartered in Mumbai.
    • Oldest exchange in Asia with ~5000 listed companies
    • Known for the benchmark index SENESEX
  2. National Stock Exchange (NSE)
    • Established in 1992, also in Mumbai.
    • India's largest exchange by volume and market capitalization
    • Benchmark index: NIFTY 50
  3. India International Exchange (India INX)
    • Located in Gujarat
    • - Provides international trading opportunities for Indian investors.
  4. NSE International Exchange (NSE IX)
    • Also in Gujarat
    • - Provides international trading opportunities for Indian investors.
  5. Metropolitan Stock Exchange (MSE)
    • Based in Mumbai.
    • Offers trading in equities, derivatives, and debt instruments.
  6. United Stock Exchange (USE)
    • - Specializes in currency derivatives trading.
